Neuschwanstein Castle

The castle was commissioned by King Ludwig II of Bavaria starting in 1869 and was never fully completed. It was designed as a romanticized monument to medieval culture and kingship, inspired by the operas of Richard Wagner. The castle is one of Europe's most popular tourist attractions, receiving approximately 1.3 million visitors annually. It is located near Hohenschwangau Castle, where King Ludwig II spent his childhood.

Altes Rathaus, Bamberg

This historic building is uniquely situated on an artificial island in the middle of the Regnitz River. The town hall was originally built in 1386. It is accessible by two bridges, the Obere Brücke and Untere Brücke. The building is adorned with intricate frescoes on its exterior walls. Bamberg's old town, including this landmark, is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
